THE ANGELS' SHARE SCANDAL: BY KILIAN INITIATES MULTIMILLION-DOLLAR LEGAL ACTION

An in-depth look into the design and chemical structure dispute shaking the luxury perfume markets.

The Industry Conflict

A big perfume war started yesterday between two famous fragrance brands[span_0](start_span)[span_0](end_span). The luxury French brand By Kilian is suing the Dubai-based brand Lattafa because of a massive design and formula copy scandal[span_1](start_span)[span_1](end_span). Legal experts note that this case marks a critical turning point in luxury intellectual property rights.

The Design Infringement

By Kilian says that Lattafa completely copied the famous bottle design of their best-selling perfume, Angels' Share[span_2](start_span)[span_2](end_span). The founder, Kilian Hennessy, designed the Angels' Share bottle to look like a luxury, heavy crystal glass[span_3](start_span)[span_3](end_span). It features deep lines on the glass and a very heavy, clear plastic cap[span_4](start_span)[span_4](end_span). Lattafa released a perfume called Khamrah in 2022, and the design is almost identical[span_5](start_span)[span_5](end_span).

The Aesthetic Evaluation

If you put the two bottles side by side, you can see these copies clearly[span_6](start_span)[span_6](end_span):

  • Both bottles are square and crafted from very heavy glass architectural elements[span_7](start_span)[span_7](end_span).
  • They both feature the same crystal-cut diamond patterns on the side walls[span_8](start_span)[span_8](end_span).
  • The caps look like large, clear ice cubes sitting on top of the structural frames[span_9](start_span)[span_9](end_span).

"This is not a coincidence," a senior representative from By Kilian stated earlier today[span_10](start_span)[span_10](end_span). "They wanted their cheap perfume to look exactly like our luxury product so they could sell millions of bottles easily.[span_11](start_span)"[span_11](end_span)

The Chemical Sourcing Leak

The scandal is not only about the bottles[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span). By Kilian also claims that a worker from their secret laboratory stole the formula of Angels' Share and gave it to Lattafa[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span). Angels' Share uses a very special and expensive mix of cognac oil, cinnamon, and oakwood[span_14](start_span)[span_14](end_span).

Scientists tested both perfumes in a lab and found out that the chemical match inside the liquid is more than 95%[span_15](start_span)[span_15](end_span).

Market Impact & Resolution

However, there is a huge price difference[span_16](start_span)[span_16](end_span). Angels' Share is a luxury perfume and costs $245, but Lattafa sells Khamrah for only $40[span_17](start_span)[span_17](end_span). Because of this, millions of people stopped buying the expensive version and started buying Khamrah[span_18](start_span)[span_18](end_span).

Lattafa Perfumes made a counter-statement today and said they are innocent[span_19](start_span)[span_19](end_span). "We did not steal anything[span_20](start_span)[span_20](end_span). Our bottle design is inspired by traditional Arabic crystal glasses, not France[span_21](start_span)[span_21](end_span). Khamrah is our own creation," the company said[span_22](start_span)[span_22](end_span). The court case will continue next month in Paris[span_23](start_span)[span_23](end_span).

Corporate Notice: If By Kilian wins the legal case, Lattafa will be legally forced to stop selling Khamrah worldwide and destroy all remaining inventory in their factories[span_24](start_span)[span_24](end_span).
