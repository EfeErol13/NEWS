THE ANGELS' SHARE SCANDAL: BY KILIAN INITIATES MULTIMILLION-DOLLAR LEGAL ACTION

An in-depth look into the design and chemical structure dispute shaking the luxury perfume markets.

The Industry Conflict

A big perfume war started yesterday between two famous fragrance brands. The luxury French brand By Kilian is suing the Dubai-based brand Lattafa because of a massive design and formula copy scandal. Legal experts note that this case marks a critical turning point in luxury intellectual property rights.

The Design Infringement

By Kilian says that Lattafa completely copied the famous bottle design of their best-selling perfume, Angels' Share. The founder, Kilian Hennessy, designed the Angels' Share bottle to look like a luxury, heavy crystal glass. It features deep lines on the glass and a very heavy, clear plastic cap. Lattafa released a perfume called Khamrah in 2022, and the design is almost identical.

The Aesthetic Evaluation

If you put the two bottles side by side, you can see these copies clearly:

  • Both bottles are square and crafted from very heavy glass architectural elements.
  • They both feature the same crystal-cut diamond patterns on the side walls.
  • The caps look like large, clear ice cubes sitting on top of the structural frames.

"This is not a coincidence," a senior representative from By Kilian stated earlier today. "They wanted their cheap perfume to look exactly like our luxury product so they could sell millions of bottles easily."

The Chemical Sourcing Leak

The scandal is not only about the bottles. By Kilian also claims that a worker from their secret laboratory stole the formula of Angels' Share and gave it to Lattafa. Angels' Share uses a very special and expensive mix of cognac oil, cinnamon, and oakwood.

Scientists tested both perfumes in a lab and found out that the chemical match inside the liquid is more than 95%.

Market Impact & Resolution

However, there is a huge price difference. Angels' Share is a luxury perfume and costs $245, but Lattafa sells Khamrah for only $40. Because of this, millions of people stopped buying the expensive version and started buying Khamrah.

Lattafa Perfumes made a counter-statement today and said they are innocent. "We did not steal anything. Our bottle design is inspired by traditional Arabic crystal glasses, not France. Khamrah is our own creation," the company said. The court case will continue next month in Paris.

Corporate Notice: If By Kilian wins the legal case, Lattafa will be legally forced to stop selling Khamrah worldwide and destroy all remaining inventory in their factories.
